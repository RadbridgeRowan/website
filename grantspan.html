GrantSpan

Find and secure funding for community resilience

GrantSpan streamlines investments in community resilience by simplifying funding program identification, matching, and grant application development.

Major disasters and long-term stressors like natural disaster risks such as floods and wildfires, bolster climate mitigation and adaptation, and promote economic prosperity and public health through job creation and the ripple effects of spending. In recent years, resilience has risen as a prominent issue on the national agenda, with passage of landmark legislation in the US supporting growing investments in community resilience. For example, FEMA's Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities (BRIC) program grow from $500 million to $2 billion between 2020 and 2022, and the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law of 2021 allocated billions towards resilience initiatives.

Despite these policy changes and increased funding opportunities, funding programs can be intricate and difficult to navigate, particularly for new and emerging resilience practitioners. The US federal government alone hosts hundreds of funding programs – each with distinct goals, requirements, and language, presenting a challenge for local applicants – who cannot become experts in all programs – particularly if agency staff are already short on time and resources.

GrantSpan is a platform tailored for both applicants and funding program administrators. It aids applicants in navigating this complex funding landscape by identifying relevant funding programs, matching them with appropriate projects, and assisting in the development of applications that are complete, data-driven, and responsive to program criteria. For program administrators seeking to leverage the benefits of tech-enabled features, GrantSpan offers a curated experience that streamlines the application process, saving valuable staff and consultant time, energy, and resources.
