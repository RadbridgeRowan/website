Parkbeat

Data-driven insights for parks and open space managers

Parkbeat harnesses big data to generate actionable insights, dynamic visualizations, and predictive analytics on visitation and economic benefits associated with recreational lands.

Parks and Open Space: Our Recreation Infrastructure

America's open spaces and public lands encompass millions of acres of public lands for recreational use, ranging from urban parks to vast wilderness. These natural environments, critical recreation infrastructure, also provide various vital co-benefits and ecosystem services like water supply and air quality. Moreover, visitors to recreation sites contribute to local economies through spending at shops, restaurants, hotels, and other establishments.

Challenges for Parks and Open Space Managers

Data is key for park and open space managers, but it also poses challenges when it comes to quantifying and understanding recreational use of their lands and the economic impact of visitation. Traditional data sources are often infrequently updated and usually presented in a static format. With the advent of big data, new possibilities have emerged. Parkbeat technology helps park managers deserve a more sophisticated toolkit.

Parkbeat: Data-Driven Insights to Support Decision Making

Parkbeat addresses this need, offering parks and open space managers a dynamic, data-driven toolkit to better manage their lands and make the case for investments in terms of co-benefits and economic impact. By integrating anonymized mobile device data with GIS mapping capabilities and interactive visualization tools, Parkbeat provides dynamic, on-demand data and insights that allow managers to understand usage, allocate resources effectively, maximizing the recreational, environmental, and equity co-benefits of their investments.
