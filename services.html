Economic Impact Analysis

Data science-powered visitation and economic impact analysis

Radbridge leverages advanced methodologies including machine learning, geospatial analysis, and mobile location data to quantify economic impacts of outdoor recreation, restoration projects, and infrastructure investments.

We combine cutting-edge data science with traditional economic analysis to provide comprehensive assessments that capture both market and non-market benefits. Our innovative approaches overcome challenges of sparse visitation data by integrating mobile location analytics with observed data and robust economic modeling.

Our methodologies provide a defensible analysis that aim to incorporate the latest advances in spatial analytics and machine learning. We also quantify non-market benefits including consumer surplus, healthcare cost reductions, and ecosystem services alongside traditional economic multiplier effects.

Our Approach

Data Science & Analytics

  • • Mobile & sensor-based visitation capture: Acquire and clean anonymized mobile-location, trail counter, and other sensor feeds
  • • Geospatial data fusion & processing: Integrate land-manager boundaries, trail networks, amenities, and environmental layers
  • • Machine-learning demand modeling: Use ML algorithms to fill data gaps, predict visit counts, and create time-series forecasts
  • • Visitor segmentation & trip profiling: Classify visits as local vs. non-local, attach trip distance, dwell time, and activity type
  • • Statistical QA/QC & validation: Apply cross-validation, hold-out testing, and benchmark comparisons for defensible estimates

Economic Impact Assessment

  • • Visitor-spending research & profiling: Triangulate literature-based benchmarks, custom surveys, and credit-card data for robust spend profiles
  • • Regional input-output modeling (IMPLAN / RIMS II): Convert direct spending into jobs, labor income, GDP/value-added, and supply-chain effects
  • • Fiscal impact analysis: Estimate state & local tax revenues plus potential infrastructure cost offsets for budget officers
  • • Non-market valuation & health/ecosystem benefits: Measure consumer surplus, avoided healthcare costs, and ecosystem-service values
  • • Interactive dashboards & data-visualization: Deliver web-based dashboards and presentation-ready graphics for scenario exploration

Benefit-Cost Analysis

Rigorous economic evaluation for informed investment decisions

Radbridge provides comprehensive benefit-cost analysis services that help organizations make informed investment decisions by rigorously quantifying project benefits against their costs.

Our team applies advanced economic methodologies to evaluate investments across sectors including hazard mitigation, infrastructure, restoration, and green infrastructure. We develop custom analytical frameworks that capture the full spectrum of benefits and costs, including ecosystem services, co-benefits, and equity considerations.

From FEMA-compliant analyses to watershed-scale optimization tools, we ensure our BCAs meet federal standards while providing actionable insights for strategic decision-making. Our work supports funding applications, policy development, and investment prioritization across multiple sectors.

  • • Federal compliance: FEMA BCA Toolkit, OMB guidelines, and agency-specific requirements
  • • Custom analytical frameworks: Tailored methodologies for unique project contexts and benefit categories

FEMA Hazard Mitigation

Expert guidance for successful FEMA mitigation funding

Radbridge has supported communities across the nation with benefit-cost analysis and strategy to access more than $40 million in FEMA funding for mitigation planning and projects.

Our team combines deep expertise in FEMA's hazard mitigation programs with innovative approaches to nature-based solutions and green infrastructure. We've led the development of FEMA's guidance for ecosystem services and green infrastructure in the BCA Toolkit, ensuring our clients benefit from the latest policy developments.

From screening-level assessments to full benefit-cost analyses, we provide comprehensive support throughout the FEMA application process. Our work has directly supported successful awards including conducting the BCA for a $37 million BRIC grant.
